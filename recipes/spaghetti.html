Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti, a classic Italian favorite, embodies simplicity and comfort in every twirl. Picture strands of perfectly cooked pasta, bathed in a luscious tomato sauce that sings with the essence of fresh herbs and garlic. Each forkful promises a delightful marriage of flavors, as the tangy tomatoes mingle with aromatic basil and the gentle heat of red pepper flakes. Whether enjoyed as a cozy family dinner or a romantic meal for two, spaghetti's timeless appeal transcends generations, offering a taste of Italy's culinary soul.

After immersing ourselves in the essence of this beloved dish, let's unravel the recipe, inviting others to experience its comforting embrace and delicious simplicity.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti: 1 pound (450g) of dried spaghetti noodles.
  • Tomato Sauce: Two cans of crushed tomatoes and one can of tomato paste.
  • Aromatics: Two cloves of garlic, minced, and one small onion, finely chopped.
  • Herbs and Seasonings: Fresh basil leaves, dried oregano, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes for a hint of heat.
  • Olive Oil: To sauté the garlic and onion and add richness to the sauce.
  • Sugar: A teaspoon of white sugar to balance the acidity of the tomatoes.
  • Parmesan Cheese: Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish and extra flavor.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Sauce: In a large saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and chopped onion, sautéing until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add Tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste, stirring to combine. Season with salt, black pepper, dried oregano, and a pinch of red pepper flakes for heat.
  3. Sweeten and Simmer: Sprinkle in a teaspoon of sugar to balance the acidity of the tomatoes. Allow the sauce to simmer gently for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  4. Cook the Spaghetti: In a separate pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti noodles and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Drain the cooked pasta, reserving a small amount of pasta water.
  5. Combine Pasta and Sauce: Add the cooked spaghetti to the tomato sauce, tossing gently to coat each strand. If the sauce seems too thick, add a splash of reserved pasta water to achieve your desired consistency.
  6. Finish with Fresh Basil: Tear fresh basil leaves and sprinkle them over the spaghetti, gently tossing to incorporate.
  7. Serve and Garnish: Divide the spaghetti among plates or a large serving platter. Grate Parmesan cheese over the top and garnish with additional fresh basil leaves, if desired.
  8. Enjoy: Serve immediately, savoring each forkful of this comforting classic. Pair with a crisp green salad and crusty bread for a complete meal.
